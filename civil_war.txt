
    <h2>Causes</h2>

    <p>
     The Civil War was primarily caused by deep-seated differences between the Northern and Southern states over issues like slavery,
     states' rights, and economic policies. 
     The Southern economy relied heavily on slavery, 
     while the North was more industrialized and opposed the expansion of slavery into new territories.
    </p>

    <h2>Key Events</h2>

    <p>
        <strong>Secession</strong>: 
        Following the election of Abraham Lincoln in 1860, 
        seven Southern states seceded from the Union and formed the Confederate States of America.
        <br>
        <strong>Attack on Fort Sumter</strong>: 
        The war officially began on April 12, 1861, 
        when Confederate forces attacked Fort Sumter in South Carolina.
        <br>
        <strong>Major Battles</strong>: 
        Notable battles included the Battle of Gettysburg, 
        the Battle of Antietam, and the Siege of Vicksburg.
        <br>
        <strong>Emancipation Proclamation</strong>: 
        In 1863, Lincoln issued the Emancipation Proclamation, 
        which declared all slaves in Confederate-held territory to be free.
    </p>

    <h2>Outcome</h2>
    <p>
      The war ended in 1865 with the surrender of Confederate General Robert E. 
      Lee to Union General Ulysses S. Grant at Appomattox Court House. 
      The Union victory preserved the United States as one nation and led to the abolition of slavery.
      </p>

    <h2>Significance</h2>
    <p>
      The Civil War was the deadliest conflict in American history, 
      with an estimated 620,000 soldiers losing their lives. It resolved the question of whether the United States would remain a unified nation and ended the institution of slavery.
    </p>
